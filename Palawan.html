Puerto Princesa Underground River

The world's second-longest subterranean river is the Puerto Princesa Underground River. It's the one that attracts thousands of tourists here and is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The drive going to the jump-off point in Sabang Wharf will treat you to a refreshing view of the rural landscape.









Tubbataha Reef

Situated in the southern region of the island is a marine sanctuary called the Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park, one of the best diving spots in the Philippines and the world This beautiful area can be found in the waters of the Cagayancillo town and is also recognized as one of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the Philippines. The whole sanctuary comprises two atolls, or ring-shaped reefs, that are home to an array of colorful aquatic animals and corals.






Kayangan Lake

Coron is a laid back destination that has plenty to offer visitors. The island has lakes, lagoons, and beautiful white-sand beaches. This tropical destination lets you relax, unwind, and get away from the daily grind. One of the best attractions in Coron island-hopping tours is no other than Kayangan Lake.









Barracuda Lake

Another popular lake attraction in Coron is Barracuda Lake. The Tagbanuas, Cuyonon, and Calmiananen were the original inhabitants of the Calmianes Islands which included Coron. The rock formations and the turquoise waters of the lake will make you stop and stare for a moment before dousing the afternoon heat by diving into it. It got its name from the fact that divers discovered the skeleton of a barracuda in its depths. Barracuda Lake is also one of the cleanest lakes in the country and a famous attraction included in an island-hopping tour in Coron.







Nacpan Beach

This powdery paradise was one of the secret getaways for some tourists until its beauty was discovered by many and became one of the most sought after beach included in El Nido island-hopping tours. Nacpan Beach is a four-kilometer strip of cream-colored sand and coconut trees with crystal clear turquoise water. It is17th kilometers north of El Nido town proper in Palawan. Tourists can lie on the beach or settle in hammocks if they want to do some sunbathing. They can also opt to rest in Nacpan's shady spots.








Port Barton

Port Barton is lovely, calm, and quiet. It's only 2-3 hours away from Puerto Princesa City, and there are fewer people here compared to its province's most famous neighbors. Joining a Port Barton day tour is worth it as the town offers numerous tourist spots perfect for any traveler! Situated 1-2 hours by land from Poblacion, this peaceful coastal town is an accessible location for international travelers. You can go here or choose to remain for a few evenings.






Honda Bay

Honda Bay is one of the first tourist spots in Palawan to have made a mark on the tourist map, with its beautiful beaches and strategic location. With a pier that's accessible from anywhere in the capital within 30 minutes, Honda Bay tours allow travelers the ease of uncovering three gorgeous sandbars: Luli Island, Cowrie Island, and Starfish Island.









Twin Lagoon

The Twin Lagoon in Coron Island, Palawan province is picturesque lagoons divided by towering limestone walls. Visiting the lagoons offers a bit of adventure. From the town proper, they are accessible only by boat, which will take you through a maze of giant karst walls. If you're eager for a hassle-free adventure, you can book a Twin Lagoon tour












John Mark De Leon's trip to Palawan

Palawan is a breathtaking province in the Philippines, known for its stunning natural beauty and rich biodiversity. Its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and towering limestone cliffs make it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. Home to the renowned Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, as well as the captivating landscapes of El Nido and Coron, Palawan offers unparalleled opportunities for exploration and relaxation. With its vibrant coral reefs, hidden lagoons, and lush rainforests, Palawan is truly a tropical gem waiting to be discovered.
The Puerto Princesa Underground River is a mesmerizing natural wonder located in Palawan, Philippines. It boasts a UNESCO World Heritage Site status and is one of the New Seven Wonders of Nature. This subterranean river stretches for over 8 kilometers beneath a captivating limestone karst landscape, showcasing stunning rock formations, intricate cave systems, and diverse wildlife. Visitors can embark on a serene boat journey through its darkened passages, illuminated by the glow of thousands of bats and unique bioluminescent organisms. It's a must-visit destination for adventurers and nature enthusiasts alike, offering a glimpse into the enchanting beauty of the underground world.
Tubbataha Reef is a pristine marine sanctuary situated in the Sulu Sea, Philippines. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its exceptional biodiversity and vibrant coral reefs, making it a paradise for divers and marine enthusiasts. Spanning over 130,000 hectares, it is home to a stunning array of marine life, including endangered species such as green sea turtles, hawksbill turtles, and various shark species. With its crystal-clear waters and kaleidoscopic coral gardens, Tubbataha Reef offers an unforgettable underwater experience, showcasing the wonders of the Philippine seas. Kayangan Lake is a hidden gem nestled amidst towering limestone cliffs on Coron Island in the Philippines. Dubbed as one of the cleanest lakes in Asia, it boasts crystal-clear turquoise waters surrounded by breathtaking scenery. Accessible via a short hike and a steep descent, the lake offers a serene oasis for swimming and snorkeling. Visitors are enchanted by its stunning beauty, with underwater rock formations and vibrant marine life visible through the transparent waters. Kayangan Lake is a must-visit destination for those seeking tranquility and natural splendor in the heart of Palawan.
Barracuda Lake is a captivating natural wonder located on Coron Island in the Philippines. Famous for its unique thermocline phenomenon, this picturesque lake features layers of warm and cold water that create surreal underwater landscapes. Divers and snorkelers are drawn to its crystal-clear waters, where they can encounter fascinating rock formations, including limestone cliffs and submerged trees. Barracuda Lake is renowned for its serene atmosphere and stunning visibility, making it a must-visit destination for adventurers seeking an otherworldly experience in Palawan.
Nacpan Beach is a pristine stretch of coastline located in El Nido, Palawan, Philippines. Known for its powdery white sand, crystal-clear waters, and lush greenery, it offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. With its picturesque scenery and laid-back atmosphere, Nacpan Beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and enjoying breathtaking sunsets. Visitors can also explore nearby viewpoints for panoramic vistas of the coastline and surrounding islands. Nacpan Beach is a hidden paradise waiting to be discovered by travelers seeking natural beauty and relaxation.
Port Barton is a charming coastal village nestled on the northwestern coast of Palawan in the Philippines. With its pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and relaxed atmosphere, it offers a peaceful retreat away from the crowds. Visitors can enjoy activities such as island hopping, snorkeling, and beachcombing, or simply unwind and soak in the stunning surroundings. Port Barton's laid-back vibe and untouched beauty make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic and tranquil beach getaway in Palawan.
Honda Bay is a picturesque bay located near Puerto Princesa in Palawan, Philippines. Renowned for its crystal-clear waters and stunning coral reefs, it's a haven for snorkelers and divers. Visitors can explore the bay's numerous islands, each offering pristine beaches and diverse marine life. Island-hopping tours are popular, allowing guests to discover beautiful spots like Pandan Island, Cowrie Island, and Luli Island. Honda Bay is a tropical paradise, perfect for those seeking sun, sea, and adventure in Palawan.
Twin Lagoon is a breathtaking natural wonder nestled in Coron, Palawan, Philippines. It consists of two stunning lagoons separated by towering limestone cliffs. During high tide, visitors can swim or kayak through a small opening beneath the cliffs to reach the inner lagoon, where the water is warmer and even more tranquil. At low tide, the two lagoons are connected, revealing a mesmerizing blend of clear turquoise and emerald waters. Twin Lagoon is a must-visit destination for its surreal beauty and serene atmosphere, offering a memorable experience for travelers exploring Palawan's wonders.
Palawan's beauty is unparalleled, captivating travelers with its pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and lush landscapes. Its natural wonders, from the underground river to the stunning limestone cliffs, create a breathtaking tapestry of sights and experiences. Whether exploring the vibrant coral reefs of Honda Bay, trekking through the verdant jungles, or simply relaxing on the shores of Nacpan Beach, Palawan offers a paradise for every traveler. Its rich biodiversity, coupled with the warmth and hospitality of its people, adds to the allure of this gem in the Philippines. Palawan isn't just beautiful; it's a testament to the awe-inspiring power of nature, leaving visitors spellbound by its splendor.
